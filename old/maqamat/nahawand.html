Maqam Nahawand

 Maqam Nahawand

 
Ajam on E
  
Nahawand on C  
Hijaz on G

 
Ajam on E
  
Nahawand on C  
Kurd on G

 


Maqam Nahawand has two versions shown above. In general the first version (Nahawand-Hijaz) is used on the way up, and the second version (Nahawand-Kurd) is used on the way down. The secondary jins is the Ajam trichord on the 3rd note, often used in modulation.

When starting on lower G, this maqam is called maqam Sultani Yakah.

 

Hear this Maqam:

Violin   Maqam Nahawand on C
Violin   Maqam Sultani Yakah on G
Oud   Maqam Nahawand on C - form 1
Oud   Maqam Nahawand on C - form 2
Buzuq   Maqam Nahawand on C
Buzuq   Maqam Sultani Yakah on G

 Maqam Farahfaza

 
Ajam on B
  
Nahawand on G  
Kurd on D

 

 

Maqam Farahfaza has the same tonal intervals as maqam Nahawand transposed on G. However its melodic development is different, and makes much use of the Ajam trichord on the third (B).
 

Hear this Maqam:

Violin   Maqam Farahfaza on G
Oud   Maqam Farahfaza on G
Buzuq   Maqam Farahfaza on G

Recording Samples:

  Bashraf Farahfaza Ismail Haqqi
  Samai Farahfaza Tanburi Jamil
  Longa Farahfaza Riyad Sunbati


 Maqam Nahawand Murassah (Sunbulah)

 
Nahawand on C
Hijaz on F

Maqam Nahawand Murassah should not be confused with form 1 of maqam Nahawand (Nahawand-Hijaz), since although they both have the same lower and upper ajnas, the starting point of the Hijaz tetrachord (which is also the dominant) is a different note.
 

Hear this Maqam:

Violin   Nahawand Murassah on C
Oud   Nahawand Murassah on C
Buzuq   Nahawand Murassah on C

Recording Samples:

  Samai Nahawand Masud Jamil


 Maqam Ushaq Masri

 
Ajam on G
  
  Busalik on D
Bayati on A

 

 

In the Busalik tetrachord, the tuning of the third note (F) is lower than the similar Nahawand tetrachord. The difference in tuning is about 1/9th of a tone (called koma in Turkish msuic).
  

Hear this Maqam:

Buzuq   Maqam Ushaq Masri on D

Recording Samples:

  Abdelwahab: Aheb Achoufak
  Simon Shaheen: Dawn oud taqsim
